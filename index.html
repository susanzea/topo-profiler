What is Topography?

Topography is a representation of the geographic features of an area. This is accomplished by collecting discrete points of elevation and then connecting points of equal elevation to form a topographic map. The lines that connect these points are called contour lines and a key feature of theirs is that they never intersect.



Profile Derivation

A topographic profile is a chart that represents the change in elevation along a crossection on a topographic map. In other words, a profile shows what that infinitesimal thin slice of terrain would look like from the side, instead of from above like a topographic map. Due to elevation data inherently consisting of discrete points, the gaps between lines must be interpolated.
